Indian Super League (ISL)

The Indian Super League (ISL) is the top-tier professional football league in India. Launched in 2014, it has rapidly become the most popular and widely watched football league in the country. With world-class players, passionate fans, and competitive clubs, the ISL is transforming Indian football.

History

The ISL was founded in 2013 and its inaugural season was played in 2014. The aim was to revolutionize football in India by bringing international standards, foreign players, and increased visibility. Initially, the ISL ran alongside the I-League but eventually became the top-tier league in the Indian football pyramid.

League Format

  • Currently features 12 teams from across India.
  • Each team plays others in a home-and-away round-robin format.
  • Top 6 teams qualify for the playoffs.
  • Final is played at a neutral venue to decide the champion.

Champions

  • ATK – 3-time champions (2014, 2016, 2019-20)
  • Chennaiyin FC – 2 titles (2015, 2017-18)
  • Hyderabad FC – Champions in 2021-22
  • Mohun Bagan Super Giant – 2022-23 winners
  • Bengaluru FC – 2018-19 champions

Records

  • Most Appearances: Mandar Rao Dessai

  • Fastest Goal: 13 seconds by Jerry Mawihmingthanga

Fun Facts

  • Founded with Bollywood stars like Ranbir Kapoor, John Abraham, and Sachin Tendulkar owning franchises.
  • Mumbai City FC became the first Indian club to play in AFC Champions League group stage.
  • Kerala Blasters has one of the most passionate fan bases in India — known as the "Manjappada".

Broadcasting & Popularity

The ISL is broadcast live across multiple platforms and languages in India. With stadiums filling up and millions of fans tuning in, it has brought football to the mainstream. It is now widely followed in rural and urban areas alike.

Future of ISL

The ISL continues to grow, with the introduction of youth leagues, women's football development, and global partnerships. With India’s rising interest in football, ISL is expected to play a key role in producing international-level players and lifting the standard of Indian football.
